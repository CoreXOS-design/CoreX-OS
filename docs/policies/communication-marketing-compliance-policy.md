## 1. Why this policy exists

South African law requires the agency to retain all client communication and to market lawfully:

- **FICA / Property Practitioners Act** — client and transaction records, including communications, must be kept for **5 years**.
- **POPIA** — personal information may only be processed lawfully, for a defined purpose, with the right consent, and kept no longer than necessary.
- **CPA (Consumer Protection Act) and its 2026 Amendment Regulations** — govern how and when we may contact consumers, including direct marketing and the forthcoming opt-out registry (NCC).

The agency carries the legal liability when these are breached — not the individual agent. This policy sets out how HFC meets these obligations through CoreX, and what each staff member must do.

## 2. The communication archive — what is captured

CoreX keeps an **immutable, independent copy** of business communications so the agency can defend itself if a client makes a claim years later.

- **Email** — business email (sent and received) is captured automatically into CoreX.
- **WhatsApp** — business WhatsApp conversations are captured automatically into CoreX via an approved capture tool on the agent's browser.
- Once captured, a communication **cannot be altered or deleted** by anyone. It is retained for **5 years**, then archived/pruned in a recorded, lawful way.

**Why this matters to you:** if you leave the agency, or you delete your own emails or WhatsApp messages, the agency still holds the record. This protects the agency *and* protects you — your conduct is on record exactly as it happened.

---

## 3. The contact rule (read this twice)

Capture is **gated to contacts that exist in CoreX**. This is deliberate and it has two sides:

### 3.1 No contact in CoreX = no capture
If the person you are communicating with is **not** loaded as a contact in CoreX, that communication is **not** ingested into the archive. Your genuinely personal, non-business communications are therefore not captured. This is your privacy guarantee, and it keeps the archive limited to legitimate business records (a POPIA requirement — we only keep what we need).

### 3.2 Every business contact MUST be loaded on CoreX
Because capture follows contacts, the integrity of the archive depends on contacts being loaded. Therefore:

> **It is a breach of this policy to conduct agency business with any person who is not loaded as a contact in CoreX.**

If you are emailing or WhatsApping a buyer, seller, attorney, or any other party about agency business, **that party must be a contact in CoreX.** Failing to load a business contact — whether by oversight or to keep a conversation off the record — is an explicit, disciplinable breach. There is no grey area here: business contact, loaded on CoreX, every time.

### 3.3 Inbound contact — you have a grace period to load
When a new contact reaches out to **us** first (an inbound email or WhatsApp from someone not yet in CoreX), the law treats this differently — they have initiated contact, so we may receive and respond to them. You then have a short grace period to load them as a contact: **4 calendar days** (this accounts for a Friday-afternoon enquiry surviving the weekend and a busy Monday).

- Load them within the window → their first message is retained in the archive from the moment they made contact.
- Don't load them within the window → that first message is not retained, **and** if it was genuine agency business, you are in breach of the contact rule above.

In short: inbound buys you a few days, not an exemption. If it's business, load them — promptly.

## 4. Outbound marketing — only approved templates

This section applies to **outbound** communication — where **we** initiate contact to market to someone. (Inbound, where they contacted us first, is covered in Section 3.3 and is normal business response, not marketing — these rules do not restrict how you reply to someone who approached you.) Direct marketing to consumers is regulated, so to keep every outbound message lawful and defensible:

### 4.1 Approved templates only on first contact
First-contact / business-initiated marketing messages (email and WhatsApp) may be sent **only** using CoreX-approved templates. You may **not** edit the template wording or compose your own first-contact marketing message. The approved templates contain the legal wording required (sender identity, purpose, opt-out, consent basis).

### 4.2 Free-form only on response
Once the contact has **replied**, you may communicate freely with them in that conversation. Your reply is a normal business exchange, not unsolicited marketing.

### 4.3 Open-stock and evidence basis
Where a template makes a market claim — for example, "we have active buyers looking for a 3-bedroom home in Uvongo" — that claim is generated from live CoreX data and a record of the supporting evidence is captured at the moment of sending (which buyers, what they are looking for, the date). Outreach about a specific property is only made where the property is **open** stock (no sole or exclusive mandate is interfered with).

### 4.4 Consent and opt-out
We honour every opt-out immediately and permanently. We do not contact anyone who has asked not to be contacted.

## 6. Your obligations — summary

1. Load **every** business contact into CoreX before conducting business with them.
2. Understand that your business email and WhatsApp are captured and retained for 5 years, and cannot be deleted from the agency's record.
3. Use **only approved templates** for first-contact marketing; never edit them or write your own.
4. Communicate freely only **after** a contact has responded.
5. Honour every opt-out immediately.
6. Never attempt to keep a business communication off the record.
